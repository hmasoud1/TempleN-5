HOW WORD TILES WORK

GRANULARITY

How the source text is chopped into pieces.

  • Word — split on spaces. Each word is one piece.
  • Phrase — split on commas, semicolons, dashes, sentence breaks.
  • Sentence — split on periods, question marks, exclamation points.
TILES

How many tiles end up in the pile.

  • 30 / 60 / 100 / 200 / 400 — random sample of pieces (cycles if text is shorter).
  • ALL — every piece in the text becomes a tile. Reveals the Group by control.
GROUP BY (ALL only)

Bundles consecutive pieces into one tile — n-grams.

  • 1 — unigrams. Every piece on its own tile.
  • 2 — bigrams. Pairs of consecutive pieces share one tile.
  • 3 — trigrams. And so on, up to 15.

Grouping happens before the shuffle, so each tile holds a coherent chunk in original order.

EXAMPLE

Tao Te Ching · Word · ALL · Group by 3 → tiles like "The Tao that", "can be trodden", "is not the", etc. — every word covered, three at a time.

TRUTHS

PILE — HOVER TO ENLARGE · DRAG TO MAT
MOVE click and drag any tile  ·  SELECT click a tile  ·  ROTATE select then press R +15° or E −15°  ·  SNAP drop near another tile (same rotation) — toggleable  ·  FREE PLACE turn off snap to drop tiles anywhere, including overlapping  ·  RETURN drag a placed tile back to the pile  ·  DESELECT press ESC
